##üîë 1. Sentence Structure (S‚ÄìV‚ÄìO Basics)
===============================================
Core Rule:
    Subject (S) + Verb (V) + Object (O)   |  This is the default structure in English.

Example:
    I   (S) eat   (V) rice   (O).
    She (S) likes (V) coffee (O).


English sentence needs at least a Subject + Verb, object is optional.
S + V (no object):
                    He sleeps.
                    They run.

S + V + O + O (two objects):
    He gave me a gift.
    She told us a story.

S + V + O + Complement (extra info about object):
    They made me happy.
    We elected him president.


Sentence Expansion (Add Details):
üëâ Rule: S + V + O + (extra info) | You can add time, place, manner ‚Üí usually after the object.

    I eat rice at night.                (time)
    She reads a book in the library.    (place)
    He drives a car carefully.          (manner)



üëâ English word order is fixed (unlike Bangla).
   Always: Subject ‚Üí Verb ‚Üí Object ‚Üí Extra


Try to make sentences with this pattern:
    S + V ‚Üí I run.
    S + V + O ‚Üí I play football.
    S + V + O + Extra ‚Üí I play football in the park.
    S + V + O + O ‚Üí He gave me a pen.



‚úÖ  If you master this, you can already communicate 60‚Äì70% of your daily thoughts in English.
    Later grammar (tenses, questions, modals) only modifies this core structure.
