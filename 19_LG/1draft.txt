------------------------------------------------------------
🔑 1. PARTS OF SPEECH
------------------------------------------------------------

1. Noun

A noun is a person, place, thing, or idea.

Examples:
• Imran is a developer.
• Dhaka is a busy city.
• Computer is expensive.
• Love is important.

------------------------------------------------------------

2. Pronoun

A pronoun replaces a noun.

Examples:
• Imran is a developer. He works hard.
• Sarah likes coffee. She drinks it every morning.
• The students are studying. They are preparing for IELTS.

------------------------------------------------------------

3. Verb

A verb shows an action or state.

Examples:
• I study English.
• She writes emails.
• They play football.
• We are happy.

------------------------------------------------------------

4. Adjective

An adjective describes a noun.

Examples:
• It is a beautiful garden.
• He bought a new laptop.
• She is an intelligent student.

------------------------------------------------------------

5. Adverb

An adverb describes a verb, adjective, or another adverb.

Examples:
• He speaks clearly.
• She runs quickly.
• They work hard.
• The exam was very difficult.




------------------------------------------------------------
🔑 2. SENTENCE STRUCTURE (S + V + O)
------------------------------------------------------------

Formula:
Subject + Verb + Object

Examples:
• I eat rice.
• She likes coffee.
• They play football.
• We learn English.
• He writes code.

------------------------------------------------------------
🔑 3. ARTICLES (A, AN, THE)
------------------------------------------------------------

A
Use before a singular noun beginning with a consonant sound.

Examples:
• a pen
• a laptop
• a book

------------------------------------------------------------

An
Use before a singular noun beginning with a vowel sound.

Examples:
• an apple
• an engineer
• an umbrella

------------------------------------------------------------

The
Use for something specific or already known.

Examples:
• Close the door.
• I cleaned the room.
• The sun rises in the east.

------------------------------------------------------------
🔑 4. TENSES
------------------------------------------------------------

Present Simple

Formula:
Subject + Base Verb (+s/es for He/She/It) + Object

Examples:
• I study English every day.
• He studies English every day.
• They play football on weekends.
• She plays football on weekends.

------------------------------------------------------------

Present Continuous

Formula:
Subject + am/is/are + Verb-ing + Object

Examples:
• I am reading a book.
• She is cooking dinner.
• They are playing football.
• We are studying English.

------------------------------------------------------------

Present Perfect

Formula:
Subject + have/has + V3 + Object

Examples:
• I have finished my homework.
• We have completed the project.
• She has written the report.
• He has started the meeting.

------------------------------------------------------------

Present Perfect Continuous

Affirmative:
Subject + have/has + been + Verb-ing + Object

Examples:
• I have been studying all morning.
• She has been working on the project.

Negative:
Subject + have/has + not + been + Verb-ing + Object

Examples:
• I have not been sleeping well.
• He has not been feeling well.

Question:
Have/Has + Subject + been + Verb-ing + Object?

Examples:
• Have you been studying all morning?
• Has she been working today?

------------------------------------------------------------

Past Simple

Formula:
Subject + Past Verb (V2) + Object

Examples:
• I visited my friend last week.
• She played football yesterday.
• They watched a movie last night.
• I went to the market yesterday.
• I saw a bird in the garden.

------------------------------------------------------------

Future (Will)

Formula:
Subject + will + Base Verb + Object

Examples:
• I will call you tomorrow.
• She will help me.
• They will arrive soon.

Use:
Quick decisions, promises, predictions.

------------------------------------------------------------

Future (Going To)

Formula:
Subject + am/is/are + going to + Base Verb + Object

Examples:
• She is going to travel next month.
• We are going to buy a house.
• I am going to start IELTS preparation.

Use:
Planned actions and intentions.

------------------------------------------------------------
🔑 6. SUBJECT-VERB AGREEMENT
------------------------------------------------------------

Rule:
The verb must agree with the subject.

Singular Subject → Singular Verb

Examples:
• He plays football.
• She studies English.
• The boy runs fast.

Plural Subject → Base Verb

Examples:
• They play football.
• We study English.
• The boys run fast.

------------------------------------------------------------
🔑 7. MODAL VERBS
------------------------------------------------------------

Can = Ability
• I can swim.

Could = Past ability / Polite request
• Could you help me?

May = Permission
• May I come in?

Might = Possibility
• It might rain today.

Should = Advice
• You should study every day.

Must = Strong obligation
• You must wear a helmet.

Will = Future
• I will call you.

Would = Polite request
• Would you like some tea?

------------------------------------------------------------
🔑 8. ACTIVE & PASSIVE VOICE
------------------------------------------------------------

Active Voice

Formula:
Subject + Verb + Object

Examples:
• The teacher teaches English.
• She wrote a letter.

------------------------------------------------------------

Passive Voice

Formula:
Object + be + V3 + by + Subject

Examples:
• English is taught by the teacher.
• A letter was written by her.

------------------------------------------------------------
🔑 9. REPORTED SPEECH
------------------------------------------------------------

Direct Speech

• She said, "I am busy."
• He said, "I will come."

Reported Speech

• She said that she was busy.
• He said that he would come.
• She said that she had finished her work.

🔑 10. DAILY PRACTICE
------------------------------------------------------------

Daily Writing
• Write 5–10 sentences every day.

Example:
• I wake up at 6 AM.
• I brush my teeth.
• I go to the office.
• I write code.
• I study English.

------------------------------------------------------------

Daily Speaking
Speak for 2–5 minutes about:
• My family
• My office
• My hometown
• My daily routine
• My future plan

------------------------------------------------------------

Daily Reading
Read for 15–20 minutes:
• BBC Learning English
• VOA Learning English
• Short news articles
• Children's stories
• IELTS passages
